About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Bioinformatics at the John Innes Centre with a strong background in computational chemistry. My PhD at Cardiff University focused on using molecular dynamics simulations to investigate proteins implicated in neurodegenerative diseases. Now, I apply my computational expertise to plant pathogen genomics, developing bioinformatics pipelines for analyzing next-generation sequencing data. My current work is centered on understanding and combating devastating crop diseases like wheat rust by providing real-time diagnostic solutions and supporting research into pathogen resistance.

Projects

MARPLE

MARPLE (Mobile And Real-time PLant disEase) is a portable diagnostics pipeline for the rapid, in-field identification of wheat rust pathogens. Packaged using Snakemake, it utilizes data from Nanopore MinION sequencing devices to provide strain-level identification and assess fungicide sensitivity directly from infected samples, facilitating timely disease management in remote agricultural settings.
